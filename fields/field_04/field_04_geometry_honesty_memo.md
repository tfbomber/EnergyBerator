# FIELD_04 Geometry Honesty Memo
**Subject:** Risks and Limitations of Project-Derived Spatial Boundaries  
**Segment:** NEUSS_NORF_01 (Norf Pilot)

## 1. Boundary Methodology
The current spatial boundary for `NEUSS_NORF_01` is a **PROJECT_DERIVED_CONVEX_HULL**. It is generated by calculating the smallest convex polygon that encompasses all 298 building footprints identified in the pilot segment dataset.

## 2. Identified Risks
- **Over-Inclusion (The "Smoothing" Effect)**: Convex hulls do not account for concave indentations. They may bridge across streets, courtyards, or peripheral vacant lots that are not part of the active segment.
- **Edge Uncertainty**: Buildings on the perimeter of the segment define the hull's vertices. Any PV asset located just outside the segment but within the "smoothed" perimeter of the hull will be incorrectly pinned as a false positive.
- **Non-Cadastral Nature**: This boundary has no legal or administrative standing. It is a mathematical approximation.

## 3. Mandatory Disclosure
All audit artifacts and downstream reports utilizing this geometry must include the following metadata:
```json
"geometry_honesty": {
  "label": "PROJECT_DERIVED_CONVEX_HULL",
  "closure_grade": false,
  "risk": "MEDIUM_OVER_INCLUSION",
  "comment": "Boundary is a mathematical envelope of 298 footprints. False positives likely on segment edges."
}
```

## 4. Policy for E1 Status
Usage of a `PROJECT_DERIVED_CONVEX_HULL` is strictly prohibited for Evidence Tier **E1**. E1 status is permanently blocked until a city-certified administrative boundary (cadastral grade) is provided.
